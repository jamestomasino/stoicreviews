About Stoicism

Stoicism is a philosophy for everyday living. It has its origins in ancient Athens where it was one of the four principal schools of philosophy. With peers like Plato and Aristotle, a man named Zeno of Citium put together a mashup of Greek thought fit for a king (or emperor). Unlike philosophy as you may understand it today, in the ancient world it was a practical science.

The goal of stoicism is to be healthy, happy, and prosperous: a state summed up in the greek word Eudaimonia. Of course the stoic way of defining health, happiness, and prosperity are all a bit unique. It's best to think of eudaimonaia as synonym of Tranquility.

The path toward tranquility is found in four characteristics: wisdom, courage, self-control, and justice. These four together are summed up in the Greek word Arete. The Greeks have a word for everything, don't they?
